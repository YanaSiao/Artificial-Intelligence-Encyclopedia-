# Introduction to Deep Learning vs Classical ML

## 📖 Theory & Core Concepts

### The AI Hierarchy
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** The broad field of creating systems that simulate intelligence.
- **Machine Learning (ML):** A subset of AI focused on algorithms that find patterns in data to make predictions.
- **Deep Learning (DL):** A subset of ML that uses multi-layered (deep) neural networks to drive breakthroughs.

### Key Differences
- **Feature Extraction:**
    - In **Classical ML**, features are typically extracted **manually** by experts.
    - In **Deep Learning**, features are found **automatically** from raw data through cascaded non-linear units.
- **Representation Learning:** DL solves the problem of high-level abstraction by building complex concepts out of simpler, nested representations.
- **Data Types:** DL is particularly effective at handling high-dimensional, noisy raw data (images, sound, text) where manual feature engineering is difficult.
